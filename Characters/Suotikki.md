In the _Kalevala_, **Suotikki** is mentioned as a name in a passage where a character invokes blessings for cattle, requesting nourishment and sustenance for them
In this context, **Suotikki**, along with **Watikki**, **Hermikki**, **Tuorikki**, and **Mairikki**, are names used to personify the cattle, each associated with specific blessings:

- **Suotikki**: Tender fodder.
- **Watikki**: Pure water.
- **Hermikki**: Abundant milk.
- **Tuorikki**: Fresh provisions.
- **Mairikki**: Flowing milk.

This poetic device reflects the agrarian culture of the time, emphasizing the importance of livestock and the desire for their well-being.
